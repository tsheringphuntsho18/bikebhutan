Cultural Route Highlights

Ride Through Living History

Bhutan is the world's last great Himalayan Buddhist kingdom — and this route takes you right through the heart of it. Every stop has a story. Your guide makes sure you leave knowing this extraordinary country far better than when you arrived.

🏔️

Start · 3,100 m (9,100 ft)

Dochula Pass — Where the Ride Begins

Arrive to one of Bhutan's most iconic panoramas. On a clear day you'll see over a dozen Himalayan peaks stretching across the horizon — including Gangkar Puensum, Bhutan's highest mountain at 7,570 m. Surrounding you are the 108 white memorial chortens (stupas), built by the Queen Mother in 2004, and the beautiful Druk Wangyal Lhakhang temple adorned with murals depicting a century of Bhutanese monarchy. Catch your breath. Then let go.

📸 Best panorama on the entire route
👨‍👩‍👧

Descent · ~2,400 m

Meet a Bhutanese Family

As you weave through forested switchbacks you'll pass small roadside villages where families live much as they have for generations. We stop to say hello. Curious children peek around doorways, mothers wave from porches, and your guide — who knows these families — translates the laughter in both directions. These small, warm moments are what travellers tell us they remember most. This is the real Bhutan, not the postcard version.

😊 The warmest people in the Himalayas
🎋

Approaching Punakha · ~1,400 m

Punakha Suspension Bridge

Cross the longest suspension bridge in Bhutan — stretching 160 metres above the turquoise Po Chhu River, draped in thousands of colourful prayer flags whose printed mantras are carried by the breeze to bless all who pass. Built in 1997 to connect remote villages to Punakha town, today it's one of those views you never want to leave. Look upriver. Look down. Let the bridge sway.

🌉 Look down — it's stunning
🌶️

Punakha Valley · ~1,300 m

The Local Farmers' Market

Down on the warm valley floor we pause at a local farmers' market where vendors arrive at dawn with what their families grew that morning. Stalls overflow with fiery red chillies, fresh mustard greens, yak-milk datshi cheese, wild ferns, and red rice straight from the Punakha paddies. Try a snack you've never heard of. Trade smiles with the auntie behind the counter. This is everyday Bhutan at its most colourful and most delicious.

🌶️ Chillies, mountain cheese, and stories
🏰

Finish · 1,200 m

Punakha Dzong — The Grand Finale

Your descent ends at arguably the most beautiful building in all of Bhutan. Built in 1637, Punakha Dzong — the "Palace of Great Happiness" — sits at the sacred confluence of the Mo Chhu and Po Chhu rivers like a vision from another world. This six-storey white fortress-monastery has been the site of royal coronations since 1907 and remains a living centre of governance and religion. Standing here after your ride, you'll understand exactly why Bhutan is called the Last Shangri-La.

👑 UNESCO Tentative World Heritage Site
